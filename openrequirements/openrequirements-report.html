Headline Conclusions

1.
REQ017–REQ020 are not user stories — they are placeholders
Public engagement, security classification, environmental impact, and post-trial close-out are flagged as absent. They cannot be implemented until they are authored.
2.
Load-bearing terms are undefined
"meaningful human control", "swarm-aware", "containment", "forensically explainable", "defensibly", "worst-credible" are used as success criteria without definitions. Two assessors will reach different conclusions on the same pack.
3.
Safety-critical outcomes are unspecified
REQ005 (geofence breach action), REQ007 (DAA encounter resolution), REQ011 (independent termination) state goals but not behaviours. Two developers could ship incompatible systems.
4.
No authentication, RBAC, or audit logging in the REQ set
The system spans applicant, regulator, panel, RAISO, RSO, and AAIB/inquiry roles, with classification-sensitive evidence. None of this is requested in REQ001–REQ020.
5.
The Workbench's own assurance is silent
There is no requirement for assuring the assurance tool — the meta-problem is not in scope of the REQ set.

Found by Dorothy — Definitions Analyst · undefined_term
CRITICAL C10 D

Detail

This is a foundational requirement (REQ002) for SORA assessment of swarm operations. Without defining what 'swarm-aware' means, SORA assessors lack clear criteria for what evidence to demand and what distinguishes a swarm SORA from a standard single-aircraft SORA. The distinction carries regulatory weight in setting SAIL and OSO requirements.

Recommendation

Define 'swarm-aware' explicitly to cover: (1) interdependencies between aircraft (e.g., coordinated de-confliction, communication dependencies), (2) failure modes of the swarm formation logic, (3) emergent behaviors not present in single-aircraft operations, (4) decision-making authority distribution. Reference JARUS SORA v2.5 guidance on multi-UAS operations if applicable.
