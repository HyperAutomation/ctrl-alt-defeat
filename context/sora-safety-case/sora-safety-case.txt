 CAA SPECIFIC OPERATIONS RISK ASSESSMENT (SORA) Operation Title: BVLOS Swarm Trial of Five RigiTech Eiger UAS in Simulated GNSS. and C2.Denied Environment Operator: CTRL-ALT-DEFEAT Location: Spadeadam Test Range, Cumbria, UK Airspace Classification: Danger Area (Segregated) Date(s) of Operation: 1-10 May 2026  1. CONCEPT OF OPERATIONS (CONOPS) 1.1 Description of the Operation The operation consists of a developmental flight trial involving a swarm of five RigiTech Eiger unmanned aircraft systems flying beyond visual line of sight (BVLOS) within the Spadeadam segregated danger area. The aircraft will execute a pre.planned mission profile incorporating intentional, simulated denial of GNSS and command.and.control (C2) communications to assess autonomous swarm resilience and contingency behaviour. 1.2 Operational Objectives Ą Validate swarm coordination under degraded navigation and communications Ą Demonstrate bounded autonomous behaviour under loss of external references Ą Collect flight data for autonomy assurance and safety analysis 1.3 Operating Environment Ą Ground environment: Uninhabited, controlled access military range Ą Air environment: Fully segregated airspace, activated danger area Ą Other airspace users: None during trial windows 1.4 Aircraft Ą Type: RigiTech Eiger Ą Quantity: 5 Ą MTOM: <25 kg each Ą Configuration: Autonomous, cooperative swarm operation 1.5 Personnel Ą Remote Pilot / Mission Supervisor Ą Swarm Systems Engineer Ą Range Safety Officer (RSO) Ą Recovery Team 1.6 Flight Phases 1. Pre.flight checks and range clearance 2. Launch and initial climb 3. Transit to operating area 4. GNSS denial initiation 5. C2 denial initiation 6. Autonomous mission continuation 7. Contingency or mission completion 8. Recovery or safe termination  2. LOSS OF GNSS AND C2 JUSTIFICATION The loss of GNSS and C2 is intentional and controlled, undertaken solely within: Ą Segregated airspace Ą Defined temporal windows Ą Pre.validated autonomous contingency envelopes The aircraft will: Ą Remain within hard geofenced boundaries Ą Transition to predefined stable behaviours Ą Terminate flight if navigation confidence thresholds are exceeded This approach is consistent with developmental flight testing guidance under CAP 722.  8.
